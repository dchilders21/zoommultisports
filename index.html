the coach

KEITH MCDONALD - HEAD COACH

EDUCATION - BS. in Exercise Physiology, MSc. Kinesiology and Applied Physiology,

COACHING EXPERIENCE - Certified Strength and Conditioning Specialist (CSCS), USATF LEVEL 2, IRONMAN certified coach, USA Master Swim Coach

Keith began his coaching career as an assistant Cross Country and Track & Field coach at the University of Colorado. Keith’s career spans more than 25 years and focuses on amateur and professional athlete development. Keith is widely regarded as one of the best development coaches in the sports of triathlon, running, cycling, and track & field.

While an assistant at the University of Colorado, the team experienced post-collegiate success unrivaled by any other NCAA distance program. Twenty-four of athletes earned professional running contracts,13 combined USA Cross Country titles and 42 berths on U.S. teams to compete at the IAAF World Cross Country Championships since 1999. While an assistant, CU graduates won 43 USATF Championships and 10 individuals representing the U.S. at the IAAF World Track & Field Championships.

After CU, Keith became the founder and co-owner of the first triathlon retail store - training facility in the Bay Area, Zoom Multisports, dedicated to delivering top-notch retail and training services to triathletes. During this time, he was the head coach of Team Zoom Multisports, one of the most successful triathlon teams in California. He later transitioned to coaching with PacWest Athletics and Zoom Performance Coaching.

As Head Coach Keith has been an integral part of the athletes he has coached in reaching their athletic goals, from qualifying numerous athletes to the Hawaii IRONMAN World Championships to the IRONMAN 70.3 World Championships. He has guided over 100 athletes to age group victories and podiums at several ITU World Triathlon Champions in addition to IRONMAN and IRONMAN 70.3 events across the United States.

Philosophy

Coaching means more to Keith than just writing training plans. I approach each athlete individually, take into account your level of performance, goals, and timelines, listen to you, build your individual competencies and hidden talents, communicate realistic goals and react quickly to changes in the plan.

Keith’s coaching philosophy is to help you get the most out of your available training time. I don’t believe in junk mileage or useless workouts. I believe every workout has a purpose and is designed to help you reach your goals. I always talk about "the process,” as a philosophy that emphasizes preparation and hard work over race results alone. It’s critical that the athlete understands how and why improvements are happening. That’s why I often tell my athletes to trust the process and the results will follow.
